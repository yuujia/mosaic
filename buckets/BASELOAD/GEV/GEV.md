### Major Announcements and Changes
- Re-issued guidance with completion of Prolec acquisition

### Quarterly KPI Assessment
- Orders continue yoy acceleration, but revenues decelerate and margins contract.

### Key KPI Drivers
- Orders accelerated: $22B or 68% yoy, compared to 56% yoy last quarter 
- Revenues decelerated: Power grew 6% yoy while wind was -24% yoy, compared to 15% yoy and -8% yoy last quarter respectively
- Operating margin contraction: Wind losses increased to $-225M vs $-61 last quarter

### Forward Trajectory
- Revenue steady: FY26 guidance of $41B - $42B organic revenue is about 9% yoy (compare to 9% last year)
- Power segment accelerates: FY26 guidance of 16-18% organic growth (compare to 9% last year) 
- Electrification segment decelerates: FY26 guidance of 20% organic growth (compare to 28% last year)
- Acquisition contribution: Guided $3B of sales from Prolec in 2026
- Margin expansion: FY26 guidance of 11-13% EBITDA margins (compare to 8% last year)

### Upside Drivers
- Orders: Equipment margins could continue to expand in '26. Conversion of slot reservation agreements already in place. Solid state transformer could convert.
- Margins: 2027 to see benefits of higher margin orders placed in 2024 and 2025
- Margins: efficiency gains in five large factories acquired in Prolec transaction 


### Risk Factors (Macro, Execution, Structural)
- Execution: Orders now being discussed for 2030 and beyond. 
- Structural: Competition from smaller turbine manufacturers
- Execution: inflation a potential headwind to  margin improvements

- Confirms secular thesis: power gen and electrification growth evident in order volumes
