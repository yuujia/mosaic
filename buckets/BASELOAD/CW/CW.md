### Quarterly KPI Assessment
- Revenues accelerate but operating margins continue slowing and are now contracting yoy; capex inflects to acceleration yoy.

### Key KPI Drivers
- Power and Process Revenues: Accelerated to 22% yoy vs 14% yoy last quarter (compared to 15% and 9% respectively for total revenues)

### Forward Trajectory
- Decelerating total revenue: Guiding 6-8% growth for FY26, compared to 12% in FY25
- Accelerating nuclear revenue: Guiding to mid-teens growth for FY26. compared to LDD growth for 2024-2026
- Operating margins expand slower: Guiding 30-60 bps expansion for FY26, compared to 120 bps last year  
- Nuclear operating margins continue expanding: Guiding 30-50 bps expansion for Naval & Power (compared to 50 bps last year) 
- Capex decelerates: Guiding ~30% growth for FY26, compared to 48% in FY25 ($110-$120M)
- Long-term goal of 15% CAGR for nuclear: $1.5B business by mid 2030s

### Upside Drivers
- Nuclear revenue: There are currently no AP1000 orders in the guidance. FERMI announced they were going to build 4, Poland and Bulgaria are close to FID, White House is proposing 10 on federal land.


### Risk Factors (Macro, Execution, Structural)
- Execution: Margins weighed by development programs in SMR, need to convert into business.
- Structural: Westinghouse competing for nuclear orders in Europe
- Structural: government policies toward nuclear energy
- Structural: 57% of total business is defense, 13% commercial aviation, 11% industrial  

### Bucket Implications
- Confirms secular thesis: nuclear business leading the way on KPIs due to plant life extension activity.
- Risk factor: government polilcies toward nuclear energy
