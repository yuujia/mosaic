# Thesis

## Thematic Color
- NGS Testing: Roughly 1/3 of sales.  Grew 22% in FY25. Primarily Compass, broad genomic profiling. PanTracer therapy selection, and RaDaR MRD growth.
- Non-NGS Testing: Roughly 2/3 of sales. Implied 4% growth in FY25. For heme and oncology, includes IHC, FISH, Flow Cytometry, cytogenics, PCR. 
- Acquisition: Pathline. NJ laboratory for pathology/morphology.  
- Therapy Selection: PanTracer Tissue. Doubled volumes in '24 and in '25. Now maybe 10% of revenues. Tissue-based biopsy for solid tumors. Launched PanTracer Pro February 2026, which integrates NGS sequencing with IHC (protein testing). 
- Therapy Selection: PanTracer LBx, the liquid biopsy version ramping in 2H26. It received MolDX coverage in March 2026 and competes with Guardant G360. A few thousand $ per test.
- MRD: RaDaR ST, ctDNA based, launched February 2026. Has MolDX for breast and head and neck. TAM of 380k patients. Submitted MolDX for 2 other indications. 
- MRD: Next gen RaDaR, aiming for MolDX submission in 2027 and clinical launch in 2028.

## Diligence Questions
- What is the majority of NEO's test volumes right now?
- They are de-emphasizing less profitable tests, which test are those? 
- What is Compass growing at? If PanTracer is doubling, could mean Compass is negative.
- Understand this RaDaR launch is between pharma and clinical? What are the differences to know?
- How are they driving price increases outside of MolDX? Average revenue per test right now is ~$500
- RaDaR ASP: what is the expected realized pricing range, and how does it compare with MRD peers?

### Major Announcements and Changes
- Issued 2026 guidance in-line with consensus

### Quarterly KPI Assessment
- Revenues decelerated. Gross margins continue to contract yoy but was offset by G&A savings.

### Key KPI Drivers
- Test volumes decelerated: 11% yoy (356k). Shifting capacity to higher value testing categories. 

### Forward Trajectory
- Revenue deceleration: FY26 guidance of $793M - $801M (9.7% yoy v 10.1% yoy last year). Targeting NGS to grow ~22%, about the same as last year. 
- Voume growth deceleration: expected to be low to mid single digits (vs HSD).
- Pricing higher: upper single digits vs LSD (mixing towards higher value tests)
- Revenue cadence: Guiding to 10% in Q1, 8-9% in Q2, 9-10% in Q3, >10% in Q4
- RaDaR ST contribution: mid-single digit millions
- EBITDA growth: 27-31% yoy or $55-57M
- EBITDA cadence: low 20s in 1H, low 30s in 2H

### Upside Drivers
- RaDar ST: 2 other indications submitted to MolDX
- PanTracer LBx: embedding only modest amount in 2H due to pre MolDX approval. Guardant G360 grew 20k per year after MolDX approval.
- PanTracer Tissue: Doubled volumes in '24 and in '25. Now maybe 10% of revenues. Launched PanTracer Pro February 2026. 

### Risk Factors (Macro, Execution, Structural)
- Structural: Pharma business expected to decline further before next generation RaDaR picks up, but it is 5% of revenue 
- Execution: Rationalizing testing capacity, losing volumes on older tests
- Execution: Portfolio mix in mature and competitive segments of the market, like broad genomic profiling

### Bucket Implications
- Confirms secular thesis: molecular testing in oncology a rapidly growing market
- Risk from competition: a lot of innovation pursuing MRD, where its ultimately a fixed patient population
- Reimbursement risk: payor reimbursement decisions (MolDX, commercial) dictate size of addressable market
