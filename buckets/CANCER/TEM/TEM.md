# Thesis

## Thematic Color
- Oncology: ~45% of sales, grew 37% in FY25 (341K tests for $1600 ASP). Mostly xT (tissue-based tumor profiling test for Dx, therapy selection, trial matching), followed by xF (blood based liquid biopsy) and xM (MRD)
- Therapy Selection: xT about 75% of oncology volume. Originally an LDT and recently got FDA approval of xT CDx (IVD for drug-linked decision). ASP of $4500 vs $2900 previously.
- Therapy Selection: xF about 25% of oncology volume. Blood-based ctDNA. Recently submitted for FDA approval. 
- MRD: xM about 1% of volume. Submitted to MolDX for CRC indication. Majority of xM volume now is done via partnership with Personalis. Working on a tumor-naive MRD
- Acquisition: Ambry, Hereditary business, ~25% of pro forma sales. 500K tests for ~$800 ASP. Testing for inherited cancer risk.     
- Data business: ~25% of sales. Data Insights licenses genomic and clinical data to pharma for identifying new drug targets, biomarkers. Data Trials services for finding patients for clinical trial
- AI competitive moat: dataset compiled from 5500 hospitals and 8500 oncologist relationships

## Diligence Questions
- How do the data agreements work revenue wise?
- Why are they gating MRD volumes when it sounds like they are happy with Personalis agreement?

### Major Announcements and Changes

### Quarterly KPI Assessment
- Revenues decelerated slightly but margins continue to expand.

### Key KPI Drivers
- Data revenue decelerated: 25% yoy vs 26% yoy last Q. Trial affected by CRO headwinds. 
- Oncology tests accelerated: 30% yoy vs 27% yoy last Q
- Oncology rev per test accelerated: $1782 vs $1594 last Q. Migration from legacy xT to xT CDx. 
- Acquisition: Hereditary sales (Ambry) inorganic contribution of 50% vs last year

### Forward Trajectory
- Revenue deceleration: FY26 guidance of $1.59B (25% yoy v 31% organic yoy last year). 
- Oncology deceleration: FY26 guidance assumes 30% yoy vs. 37% last year 
- Hereditary deceleration: FY26 guidance assumes high teens growth yoy vs 26% last year 
- Data deceleration: Expects Insights to grow 30% yoy vs 38% last year, offset by CRO business
- Operating margins worsen: FY Guidance of ~4% EBITDA margins is about -5% operating margins, compared to -4% last year. "Investment year"

### Upside Drivers
- Oncology pricing: $500 upside from migrating to xT CDx, and commercial payers. xF approval more of a 2027 story
- Data: 126% net revenue retention, they are testing a foundational model for Astra Zeneca

### Risk Factors (Macro, Execution, Structural)
- Structural: Data business is adjacent to oncology but not tied to testing. Tied to pharma R&D which can be lumpy. 
- Execution: Migrating from legacy xT to xT CDx. Expecting "vast majority" by end of year
- Execution: testing volume, particularly on hereditary side

### Bucket Implications
- Confirms secular thesis: molecular testing in oncology a rapidly growing market
- Risk from competition: a lot of innovation pursuing what is ultimately a fixed patient population
- Reimbursement risk: payor reimbursement decisions (MolDX, commercial) dictate size of addressable market
