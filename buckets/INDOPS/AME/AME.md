# Thesis

## Thematic Color
- Test and Measurement: Electronic Instruments Group (EIG), ~2/3 of sales, most directly related to Industrial Operations. Serves process, power, industrial and aerospace. 
- Parts and MRO: Electromechanical Group (EMG), ~1/3 of sales, is designed into plant design and equipment manufacturing
- Industrial Operations: About 75% of the overall business
- Industrial Operations: EIG is short cycle, tends to follow EMG (long cycle) on a lag.
- Acquisitions: In 2025, acquired FARO Technologies (3D measurement for construction, manufacturing) and Kern Microtechnick (precision parts machinery) for $1B for $400M in sales

### Major Announcements and Changes

### Quarterly KPI Assessment
- Revenues continued accelerating, margin shrinkage partially offset operating profit acceleration

### Key KPI Drivers
- Organic growth accelerated: Both EIG (2% yoy vs 0% yoy last q) and EMG (14% vs 12%).
- EMG growth accelerated: delivery of long cycle projects
- Test and measurement margins shrink: 23% vs 25% last Q. 

### Forward Trajectory
- Revenue acceleration: FY26 guidance of mid-to-high single digit growth (vs 6.6% yoy last year), organic sales low-to-mid single digits
- Test and measurement acceleration: EIG follows EMG's acceleration   
- Margin expansion: FY26 guidance implies 30 bps of margin expansion. 35% incremental. Q1 to see yoy shrinkage, would be trough.

### Upside Drivers
- Test and measurement acceleration: EIG represents short cycle activity after recent acceleration in long cycle activity
- Margin expansion: did 45% incremental in Q4, but only guiding to 35% for FY26 because FARO is contributing mid 20s for Q1
- Acquisitions: Have $5B in capital available while maintaining IG.

### Risk Factors (Macro, Execution, Structural)
- Structural: Healthcare is about 21% of their business, grew low double digits in Q4, high single digits for FY25, facing harder comps in 2026. 
- Macro: Inflation. Pricing was about 50 bps ahed of tariffs plus inflation in Q4
- Macro: Process Industrials. began 2025 negative growth, turned positive by Q4, expecting low-to-mid single digits in FY26
- Macro: Aerospace and Defense. low double digit growth in Q4, expecting high single digits in 2026
- Macro: Power. mid-single digits in Q4, expecting mid single digits in FY26
- Macro: Automation and Engineered Solutions: low double digit in Q4, expecting mid-siingle digits in FY26 

### Bucket Implications
- Confirms secular thesis: long cycle activity accelerating across industrials, short cycle (operations) should follow.
- Risk from macro: industrial long cycle and short cycle demand
